Overview. Hellcorp Inc. provides professional services that are custom-built for each engagement. Due to the bespoke nature of our work, refunds are handled differently than standard retail purchases. This policy explains when refunds are available, how they are calculated, and the process for requesting one. This policy is governed by US federal and state law.

02 NON-REFUNDABLE ITEMS

  • Third-party costs — licenses, APIs, cloud hosting, domain registrations, SSL certificates, or any costs paid directly to third-party providers.
  • Expedited/rush fees — premium charges for accelerated timelines are non-refundable once work begins.
  • Travel and on-site expenses — flights, hotels, per diem, and related costs for on-site engagements.
  • Subcontractor costs — fees paid to engaged specialists or subcontractors.
  • Deposits for future phases — advance payments for project phases not yet started are refundable, but deposits designated as non-refundable in a signed SOW are not.
  • Training and workshops — once conducted, no refund. Cancellation policy per §01 above applies.

03 REFUND PROCESS

3.1 Request Submission

  • Submit refund request in writing to billing@hellcorp.io with: project name, invoice reference, amount requested, and detailed justification.
  • Requests must be submitted within 30 days of the triggering event (invoice date, milestone completion, or service cancellation).
  • Late requests may be denied at Hellcorp's sole discretion.

3.2 Review & Assessment

  • Hellcorp acknowledges receipt within 3 business days.
  • Assessment completed within 15 business days, which may include: review of project records, timesheets, communications, SOW terms, and delivery evidence.
  • Hellcorp may request additional information or a clarification call.

3.3 Decision & Communication

  • Written decision sent via email, including: approval/denial, refund amount (if approved), calculation methodology, and payment timeline.
  • If denied: reasons provided with reference to specific Terms of Use or SOW provisions.

3.4 Payment

  • Approved refunds processed within 15 business days of decision via original payment method.
  • If original payment method unavailable, refund via wire transfer or check within 30 business days.
  • Hellcorp does not charge refund processing fees unless the original payment was made by credit card, in which case the payment processor's refund fee (typically 2-3%) is deducted.

04 DISPUTE RESOLUTION

If a refund request is denied and the Client disagrees:

  • Escalation: Request escalation to Hellcorp's VP of Operations within 10 business days of denial. VP review within 10 business days.
  • Mediation: If escalation does not resolve the dispute, either party may request non-binding mediation. Costs split equally.
  • Arbitration: Per Terms of Use Section 13 — binding arbitration under AAA Commercial Rules in New York, NY. This is the exclusive remedy; small claims court is also available for claims within the applicable small claims limit.

Important: Filing a credit card chargeback without first following this dispute resolution process may constitute a material breach of the Terms of Use and may result in immediate suspension of all services and account termination.

05 FORCE MAJEURE & PROJECT DISRUPTION

If a project is disrupted due to force majeure events (pandemic, natural disaster, government action, cyberattack on critical infrastructure):

  • Neither party is liable for delays or non-performance during the force majeure period.
  • Hellcorp will credit the Client for any prepaid but undelivered Services during the disruption period.
  • If the disruption exceeds 60 consecutive days, either party may terminate without penalty. Prepaid amounts for undelivered work are refunded within 30 days minus any non-recoverable third-party costs already incurred.

06 CANCELLATION BY HELLCORP

If Hellcorp cancels an engagement for any reason other than Client breach:

  • Full refund of all prepaid amounts for undelivered work.
  • All completed Deliverables transferred to Client with appropriate licenses.
  • Reasonable assistance in transitioning the project to another provider (up to 10 hours, included at no cost).

EU Consumer Rights. For EU Consumers, statutory withdrawal rights under Directive 2011/83/EU apply where applicable. For B2B engagements, the refund terms below govern. Controller: Hellcorp Ireland Ltd., Dublin 4, Ireland.

01 CONSUMER WITHDRAWAL RIGHT (DISTANCE CONTRACTS)

1.1 Applicability

Under Directive 2011/83/EU (implemented via national law in each member state), Consumers have a 14-day withdrawal period for distance contracts. However:

  • Custom-made goods or services expressly made to the Consumer's specifications are exempt from the withdrawal right per Article 16(c) of the Directive.
  • Hellcorp's services are, by nature, custom-made to Client specifications. The withdrawal right therefore does not apply to the core service delivery.

1.2 Where Withdrawal Applies

If any component of the service is not custom-made (e.g., a pre-built template, off-the-shelf software license, or non-customized workshop):

  • 14-day withdrawal from the day of contract conclusion (or delivery, whichever is later per national implementation).
  • Full refund within 14 days of withdrawal receipt.
  • If service began during withdrawal period with Consumer's express consent: proportionate amount for services actually provided.
  • Refund via same payment method as original transaction, unless Consumer agrees otherwise.

03 DIGITAL CONTENT (DIRECTIVE 2019/770)

Where Deliverables qualify as "digital content or digital services" under the Digital Content Directive:

  • Conformity: Deliverables must conform to the contract, including any agreed specifications, functionality, and interoperability requirements.
  • Non-conformity remedies: If Deliverables do not conform — Client may request: (a) price reduction; or (b) termination with full refund, per the hierarchy in Article 16 of the Directive. Hellcorp has the right to cure first within a reasonable period.
  • Update obligation: Where the contract includes an update obligation, Hellcorp must provide updates for the period specified in the SOW.
  • Personal data as consideration: Where personal data is provided as consideration (counter-performance) for the digital service, the Client has additional rights including termination if Hellcorp fails to process data in accordance with the agreement.

Global Refunds. This section covers refund provisions for UK, Canada, Australia, Japan, Singapore, South Korea, Brazil, India, UAE, and other jurisdictions. US framework applies as default unless modified below or by mandatory local law.

01 JURISDICTION-SPECIFIC REFUND RULES

United Kingdom

  • Consumer Rights Act 2015: 14-day cancellation for distance contracts. Custom-made services exempt (s.28(1)(c)).
  • Goods: 30-day right to reject faulty goods, refund within 14 days of rejection. Not applicable to services per se.
  • Services: right to price reduction or repeat performance for substandard services (s.50–54 CRA). Full refund if repeat performance impossible or not provided within reasonable time.
  • Consumer Contracts (Information, Cancellation and Additional Charges) Regulations 2013: cancellation information must be provided. Failure to provide may extend the 14-day cancellation period.
  • B2B: US framework applies with UK modification — no admin fee deductions (per unfair contract terms principles under CRA 2015 s.62).
  • Governing law: England & Wales.

Canada

  • No federal statutory cooling-off period for B2B professional services. Provincial consumer protection legislation varies:
  • Ontario: Consumer Protection Act, 2002 — 10-day cooling-off for certain agreements (not custom services). False/misleading representations give rise to refund claims under s.18.
  • Quebec: Consumer Protection Act (R.S.Q. c. P-40.1) — contracts for services not subject to cancellation if custom-made. Three types of remedies for non-conformity: forced performance, replacement, or rescission (resiliation) with refund.
  • British Columbia: BPCPA — no specific cooling-off for professional services. Unfair practices give rise to damage claims including refund.
  • B2B: US framework applies. No admin fee exceeding actual documented costs.

Australia

  • Consumer guarantees under Australian Consumer Law (ACL): services must be provided with due care and skill, fit for specified purpose, and delivered within reasonable time. If not — Consumer may: (a) cancel and obtain full refund; (b) claim compensation for drop in value; (c) seek repair/repeat performance.
  • No statutory cooling-off period for professional services (cooling-off applies to door-to-door and telemarketing sales per s.69 ACL).
  • Major failure → Consumer can reject and claim refund. Minor failure → Hellcorp may remedy within reasonable time.
  • B2B: ACL guarantees do not apply if goods/services acquired for $100,000+ (s.2A ACL threshold). Below threshold, ACL applies.
  • ACCC enforcement: accc.gov.au

Japan

  • Specific Commercial Transactions Act: no cooling-off for custom-made services (Art. 58 excludes custom-ordered goods).
  • Civil Code (Amended 2020): contract termination for breach with refund of consideration (Art. 541–543). Right to demand performance, compensation, or cancellation.
  • Consumer Contract Act: clauses that unreasonably disadvantage Consumers are void (Art. 10). Disproportionate cancellation fees likely void.
  • B2B: US framework applies. Refund deductions must reflect actual costs; penalty-like deductions may be void under general contract principles.

Singapore

  • Consumer Protection (Fair Trading) Act (CPFTA): no cooling-off for professional services specifically. Consumers may claim for unfair practices (misrepresentation, false claims, etc.) via Small Claims Tribunal or court.
  • Common law: breach of contract gives right to damages. Where breach is repudiatory, innocent party may terminate and claim refund of prepaid amounts.
  • B2B: US framework applies. No deduction exceeding actual documented costs.

South Korea

  • Act on Consumer Protection in Electronic Commerce, etc.: 7-day cooling-off for online purchases (Art. 17). Exempt: custom-made goods/services per Art. 17(5).
  • Framework Act on Consumers: right to seek dispute resolution via Korea Consumer Agency (KCA) or local consumer centers.
  • Civil Act: breach → termination (Art. 543) with restitution. Refund of consideration for unperformed portion.
  • B2B: US framework applies. Korean courts may reduce or void penalty-like cancellation fees per general equity principles.

Brazil

  • CDC (Consumer Defense Code, Law 8.078/1990): 7-day反思期 (cooling-off) for off-premises/online sales (Art. 49). Custom-made services may be exempt if the Consumer specifically requested customization (Art. 49, sole paragraph interpretation by case law).
  • Non-conformity: Consumer may demand: (a) re-performance; (b) price reduction; (c) contract rescission with refund (Art. 20). Rescission with refund is the Consumer's choice, not the provider's.
  • Administrative sanctions by ANPD/PROCON for refusing legitimate refund claims.
  • B2B: Civil Code provisions govern. US framework applies with limitation: no cancellation fee exceeding actual demonstrable costs.

India

  • Consumer Protection Act, 2019: product liability for deficient services (Ch. VI). Consumer can file complaint with District/State/National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission for refund.
  • No statutory cooling-off for B2B professional services. B2C e-commerce rules (Consumer Protection (E-Commerce) Rules, 2020) provide for cancellation where applicable — custom services exempt.
  • B2B: US framework applies. Indian Contract Act, 1872: breach gives right to damages and restitution. Cancellation fees that are punitive (not genuine pre-estimate of loss) may be struck down as penalty clauses per Section 74.

United Arab Emirates

  • Federal Consumer Protection Law No. 15/2020: Consumer right to refund for defective goods/services. Custom services may be exempt.
  • DIFC: Contracts Law DIFC Law No. 6/2004 — breach gives right to damages and termination with restitution. Penalty clauses subject to DIFC court discretion.
  • ADGM: Similar contract law framework. Unfair contract terms provisions for consumers.
  • B2B: US framework applies. Penalty-like deductions subject to challenge under DIFC/ADGM contract law principles.

02 CURRENCY & PAYMENT METHOD

  • Refunds are processed in the original payment currency unless otherwise agreed.
  • Exchange rate applied: the rate on the date the refund is processed, not the date of the original payment. Client bears exchange rate risk.
  • International wire transfer fees: deducted from refund amount unless the refund was necessitated by Hellcorp's error, in which case Hellcorp bears the fees.

03 TAX IMPLICATIONS

  • Refunds may affect prior tax filings. Hellcorp issues credit notes or amended invoices as required by local tax law.
  • EU: VAT credit notes issued per Member State VAT directive requirements.
  • UK: VAT credit notes within 30 days per UK VAT Regulations 1995.
  • Australia: GST adjustment on Business Activity Statement (BAS).
  • India: GST credit note within prescribed time per CGST Rules.
  • Client is responsible for adjusting their own tax filings. Hellcorp provides supporting documentation upon request.
